When New York built big

A century of large-scale housing

From the philanthropic tenements of 1898 to Co-op City's 15,372 units, New York City repeatedly built housing at a scale that today's lot-by-lot development cannot match. The proposed Sunnyside Yard development — 12,000 to 15,000 units on 180 acres — would belong on this list.

Methodology

Source: Lynne B. Sagalyn for Vital City, "Time to Think Big Again." Project unit counts, dates, and acreage as cited by Sagalyn, cross-referenced against New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission designation reports where the project is a designated landmark or historic district. Sunnyside Yard figures from the Sunnyside Yard Master Plan (March 2020) by the New York City Economic Development Corporation.

How to read this: Each bar shows the residential unit count for one large-scale New York City housing project. Several projects (Amalgamated Cooperative, Clinton Hill, Fresh Meadows) were built in phases over multiple decades; the date shown is the construction span Sagalyn cites. Sunnyside Yard is shown as a striped range because the master plan envisions 12,000 to 15,000 units; the bar uses the midpoint.

Limitations: Unit counts represent originally built capacity, not present-day occupancy; some projects have been combined, demolished, or partially redeveloped. Sunnyside Gardens (1924–28, ~600 buildings on 77 acres) is omitted from this chart because Sagalyn cites it by building count rather than dwelling units, and the two are not directly comparable. The list follows Sagalyn's selection and is not exhaustive of every large New York City project.
